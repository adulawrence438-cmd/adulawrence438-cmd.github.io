Academic Research

Examining the Technical Challenges Biomedical Engineering Students Encounter During Clinical Training and Their Impact on Professional Development in Ghana

Background: Clinical training is a cornerstone of Biomedical Engineering (BME) education, bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge and professional practice. In Ghana, while BME programs have expanded across institutions like KNUST, UG, and ATU, the effectiveness of training is often hindered by resource constraints and equipment functionality.

Problem Statement

Despite the critical need for competent engineers, students face persistent challenges including limited access to functional equipment, inadequate supervision, and restricted hands-on practice. These factors hinder the acquisition of skills in troubleshooting, calibration, and maintenance, reducing overall professional readiness.

Research Objectives

  • Assess availability and functionality of medical equipment during clinical training.
  • Identify specific technical challenges encountered in healthcare settings.
  • Analyse impact on skill acquisition, confidence, and professional readiness.

Significance of Study

This research provides evidence-based recommendations for the Ministry of Health and GTEC to improve resource allocation, strengthen university-hospital partnerships, and support Sustainable Development Goal 3 (Good Health and Well-being) in Ghana.
