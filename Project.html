BME Clinical Training Challenges in Ghana

Academic Research

Research Objective: To examine technical challenges encountered by Biomedical Engineering students during clinical training and their impact on professional development in Ghana.

120 Students Surveyed
65% Lack Hands-on Practice
62% Device Operation Struggles
60% Reduced Confidence

Abstract Highlights

This descriptive cross-sectional study across four universities revealed significant gaps in BME education. Key findings indicate that limited access to functional equipment (55%) and inadequate supervision (58%) result in competence gaps, leaving 54% of students feeling unprepared for real-world practice. The research advocates for structured mentorship and university-hospital partnerships to ensure Ghana produces engineers ready for the healthcare delivery frontline.
